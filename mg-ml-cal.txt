mg to mL Conversion Formula
Universal Formula (All Liquids)
mL = mg ÷ density (mg/mL)
This single formula covers every liquid. The only variable that changes is the density.
For Water
Water density = 1000 mg/mL
mL = mg ÷ 1000
Example: 5000 mg of water = 5000 ÷ 1000 = 5 mL
For Medicine
mL = mg ÷ concentration (mg/mL)
Example: 500 mg dose, medicine label reads 250 mg/5 mL (= 50 mg/mL) 500 ÷ 50 = 10 mL
Note: If your label shows mg per 5 mL, divide first. Example: 250 mg/5 mL = 50 mg/mL. Then apply the formula.

Weight-Based Dosing — mg/kg to mL Calculator
Doctors often prescribe pediatric medications by body weight (mg per kg). Here is how to calculate the correct volume.
Step 1: Total dose (mg) = prescribed dose (mg/kg) × body weight (kg) 

Step 2: Volume (mL) = total dose (mg) ÷ concentration (mg/mL)
Example: Child weighs 20 kg. Doctor prescribes 10 mg/kg ibuprofen. Concentration: 100 mg/5 mL (= 20 mg/mL).
Total dose = 10 × 20 = 200 mg
Volume = 200 ÷ 20 = 10 mL

Advanced Concentration Conversions
mg/mL to Molarity
Formula: Molarity (M) = (mg/mL × 1000) ÷ molecular weight (g/mol)
Example: Protein at 5 mg/mL, molecular weight 50,000 g/mol = (5 × 1000) ÷ 50,000 = 0.1 mM
Used in biochemistry, pharmaceutical research, and lab settings.
mg/mL to PPM
Formula: ppm = mg/mL × 1000
Example: 0.5 mg/mL = 500 ppm
mg/mL to Percentage (% w/v)
Formula: % w/v = mg/mL ÷ 10
Example: 5 mg/mL = 0.5% w/v
mg/mL to µg/mL
Formula: µg/mL = mg/mL × 1000
Example: 2 mg/mL = 2000 µg/mL

Why mg and mL Are Not the Same
Milligrams (mg) measure mass. Milliliters (mL) measure volume. You cannot convert one to the other without knowing the density of the specific substance.
1 mL of water = 1000 mg. But 1 mL of oil = only 915 mg. Same volume, different mass. This is why the calculator always needs density or concentration as an input — it is the connecting factor between weight and volume.
In medicine, this distinction is critical. Using the wrong concentration in a mg to mL drug conversion can lead to an incorrect dose. Always verify the concentration on the label before calculating.

Frequently Asked Questions
How do I convert mg to mL?
Divide milligrams by the density or concentration of the liquid in mg/mL. For water, divide by 1000. For medicine, divide by the concentration shown on the label. Use the mg to mL conversion calculator above for instant results.
Is 1 mg equal to 1 mL?
No. For water, 1 mg = 0.001 mL. For other liquids, the value depends on their density. Never assume 1 mg = 1 mL without checking the substance first.
How many mL is 500 mg?
For water: 500 mg = 0.5 mL. For a medicine with 50 mg/mL concentration: 500 mg = 10 mL. The answer always depends on the substance density or medication concentration.
How do I use this calculator for a syringe dose?
Enter your prescribed dose in mg and the concentration from the medicine label. The mg to mL dosage calculator for syringe calculates the exact mL to draw. Example: 75 mg ÷ 50 mg/mL = 1.5 mL.
Can I use this calculator for any medication?
Yes, as long as you know the concentration on the bottle. Enter that concentration into the medicine field and input your dose in mg. Always confirm the result with your pharmacist or doctor before administering any medication.
How do I convert mg/mL to molarity?
Use: Molarity = (mg/mL × 1000) ÷ molecular weight (g/mol). This is commonly used in lab and pharmaceutical settings when working with protein solutions or chemical compounds.
What is the difference between mg/mL and % concentration?
mg/mL measures mass per unit volume directly. Percentage (% w/v) expresses grams per 100 mL. To convert: divide mg/mL by 10 to get % w/v. Example: 20 mg/mL = 2% w/v.
